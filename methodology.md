Methodology
Objective

The objective of this assignment was to identify companies that closely match DeepThought's Ideal Customer Profile (Federer ICP) using a structured and evidence-based research approach.

Scope
City Chosen: Hyderabad
Industry Segments:
Specialty Biotech
Specialty Chemicals
Medical Devices
Precision Engineering
Industrial Electronics

These industries were selected because Hyderabad is a major manufacturing and innovation hub with a strong presence of technology-driven companies.

Research Process

The research was carried out in five stages:

1. Company Identification

An initial list of companies was created using publicly available business directories, company websites, industry reports, LinkedIn, and manufacturing databases.

2. Eligibility Screening

Each company was screened against the basic eligibility criteria.

Manufacturer or product company
Indian operations
Relevant industry segment
Availability of sufficient public information
Approximate revenue within the target range

Companies that clearly failed these criteria were excluded.

3. Evidence Collection

For each shortlisted company, information was collected regarding:

Products and manufacturing capabilities
Leadership
Revenue band
Growth initiatives
Manufacturing facilities
Certifications
Public announcements
Expansion activities

Only publicly available information was considered.

4. Federer Scoring

Each company was evaluated using the Federer framework provided in the assignment.

The following parameters were considered:

Producer status
Accessibility
Product differentiation
Decision-maker quality
Industry growth potential
Company growth signals
Systems maturity
Leadership strength

Based on these parameters, every company was assigned a Federer Score and classified into the appropriate band.

5. Final Shortlist

The final shortlist contains companies that best satisfy the Ideal Customer Profile and demonstrate strong technical capabilities, structured operations, manufacturing excellence, and growth potential.

Research Sources

The primary sources used include:

Official company websites
Company annual reports
LinkedIn company pages
Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)
Industry reports
News articles
Investor presentations
Government and regulatory websites
Conclusion

A structured research methodology ensured that every shortlisted company was evaluated consistently using the same framework. This approach improved the quality of the shortlist and reduced subjective decision-making while identifying companies that closely align with DeepThought's target customer profile.
