Part B
Q1. How would you identify 1,000 similar companies across India?

To identify 1,000 companies matching the Ideal Customer Profile, I would combine multiple reliable public data sources instead of depending on a single database.

Primary Sources
Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA)
Startup India database
LinkedIn Company Search
IndiaMART
India Brand Equity Foundation (IBEF)
Invest India
Export Promotion Councils
Industry Associations (CII, FICCI, FIEO)
Company websites
Google Maps and Google Search
Annual reports and investor presentations
Research Process
Select target industries.
Identify manufacturing companies in each industry.
Verify manufacturing operations.
Estimate the revenue band.
Collect leadership information.
Evaluate growth signals.
Score companies using the Federer framework.
Create the final shortlist.

This approach combines multiple sources to improve accuracy and reduce bias.

Q2. How would you scale this to 1,000 companies in one month?

To build a database of 1,000 companies within one month, I would use a combination of automation and manual verification.

Week 1
Identify target industries.
Create research templates.
Build an initial company database.
Week 2
Collect company details using company websites, LinkedIn, MCA, and industry directories.
Organize data in spreadsheets.
Week 3
Apply the Federer scoring framework.
Verify company information.
Remove companies that do not satisfy the required criteria.
Week 4
Perform quality checks.
Complete documentation.
Prepare the final shortlist and reports.
Tools
Microsoft Excel
Google Sheets
LinkedIn
Company websites
MCA portal
Google Search
AI-assisted research
CRM tools for organizing information
Quality Assurance

To maintain quality:

Verify information using more than one source.
Record evidence for every important decision.
Apply the same scoring framework to every company.
Review the final list before submission.

This process allows efficient scaling while maintaining consistency, transparency, and accuracy.
